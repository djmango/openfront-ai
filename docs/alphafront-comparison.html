AlphaFront vs openfront-ai

Prior-art notes, 2026-07-06. AlphaFront = josh-freeman/openfront-rl (HF: mischievers/openfront-rl-agent, "v19b"). A fork of the OpenFrontIO game repo with an rl/ directory bolted on; trained on a SLURM cluster. Sources: their model card, training_log.json, env.py, env_server.ts, train.py.

The one-line difference They made the agent blind and moved all spatial intelligence into hand-coded heuristics; we are teaching the agent to see. Their bet buys fast plumbing and quick wins vs easy bots; ours buys a higher skill ceiling — nothing in their design can learn geography, chokepoints, or nuke aim.

Assessment

What they proved (useful to us)

  • PPO converges on the real engine with a 17-way action space — their agent goes from 2% to ~40% survival within ~200k steps.
  • 10 ticks per decision step is enough cadence to win games — same cadence we chose independently.
  • Scalar-only obs trains fast (~2,900 steps/sec) — a baseline for judging our AE-encode overhead.
  • Win-rate-gated opponent curriculum works in this game; their stage table (difficulty × nations × tribes) is directly reusable.

Where their design caps out

  • Geography-blind: no coastlines, chokepoints, territory shape, or fallout. The policy cannot learn what its heuristics hard-code (nuke aim = fixed rule, never improves).
  • Max 16 neighbor targets; big FFA maps have more relevant players than that.
  • Zero diplomacy: cannot ally, betray, donate, or embargo — a huge part of how humans win public games.
  • Only published eval is Easy/2-opponent bots; training log shows survival plateauing around 35-47% mid-curriculum.

Bottom line They are ahead on plumbing (a trained, deployed agent exists; ours hasn't finished a run) and behind on ceiling (blind + no diplomacy + heuristic tile choice). Our human-replay pipeline is the clearest differentiator: they have no imitation signal at all, and behavior cloning from real players is exactly what their scalar observation could never absorb.
