Where things stand

Two PPO runs are live on RTX 5090 pods: ppo_v2c (restarted on the new curriculum) and ppo_v3. Both have produced genuine engine wins on stage 0 (1v1 vs one nation on Onion) — the first time the win condition has actually fired, partly because the curriculum now makes wins reachable and partly because win detection was silently broken until Jul 6 (see bugs). A parallel experiment is training a behavior-cloning warm start from archived human games. The full observe→act→reward loop, the 7-map win-gated curriculum, restart-proof cloud training, and a complete visualization suite (real-client replay videos with a live model-debug overlay, plus live play against the agent) are all in place.

Observation: compress the map, bypass the rest

The core architectural finding of the project so far. Three iterations:

VersionIdeaOutcome
v1AE over tile ownership + terrain only Worked; too narrow for a full agent.
v2One unified AE over all state (tiles, players, units, diplomacy) Spatial recon excellent, but tiny exact facts fought the bottleneck: alliance pairs peaked at F1 0.67, relative troop ordering 0.81, across four tuning attempts (loss weights, pooled-vector capacity, per-slot latents, graph-to-encoder).
v3 (current)Compress only what is big: the spatial map. Everything small and exact bypasses the latent, raw Tile accuracy 99.4% (border-tile 87–99% depending on map/crowding); static structures precision 1.0 / recall 1.0 every class. Exact state is exact by construction.
Lesson: a one-bit fact reconstructed at 95% is strictly worse than reading the bit. Autoencoders are for high-dimensional state. If the policy could just consume the value directly, never make it fight dozens of map channels for latent capacity. The bypass carries pairwise diplomacy, per-player scalars, transient units (nukes in flight with impact points, transports, warships), attack aggregates, and legality masks.

Other AE findings that held up: border-weighted cross-entropy (borders are what matter strategically and blur first), owner-ID relabeling to static per-game spawn slots (any player count, fixed channels), fully convolutional training on random crops so one model serves every map size, and rarity-weighted BCE detection for structures rather than count regression — count MSE collapses to all-zeros on 99.9%-empty grids.

Action space

Full intent surface from day one — legality masking only, no curriculum masking of actions (grounded in openfront/src/core/Schemas.ts; see DESIGN.md for the complete table). Factorized masked heads over a shared conv trunk:

HeadChooses
action typenoop, attack, expand, boat, build, launch_nuke, alliance request/reject/break, donate gold/troops, embargo, retreat
player pointertarget player slot (attention over player embeddings)
tile-region pointer16×16 map region (global index space sized to the largest map; converted to batch-local indices under dynamic padding)
build / nuke typestructure or warhead class
quantitytroop fraction buckets

Masks come from exact engine legality calls each decision step, not approximations — the bridge asks the game, the policy can only pick what the engine would accept.

Reward design

Iterated from raw occupancy to a composite strength index: 0.45·land share + 0.25·military share + 0.30·economic share. Land alone mis-scores legitimate strategies (tiny-island gold-stacking economies).

  • Dense term: w_str · strength · timeweight, timeweight ramping 0.5→1.0 over the first 8000 ticks — being strong late is worth double being strong early, and unlike pure deltas it doesn't net to zero when the agent dies.
  • Delta term: small immediate credit for strength gains.
  • Terminal placement: w_place · place−0.7 — a power law, so 1st ≈ w_place, 2nd ≈ 0.62×, 10th ≈ 0.2×. Placement counts the dead correctly (rank behind everyone still alive).
  • Win bonus: w_win = 30 (doubled when the curriculum became win-gated) for an outright engine win.
  • Death penalty: small and flat.

Curriculum

Eleven stages over seven maps (Onion → Pangaea → Caucasus → BlackSea → BetweenTwoSeas → World → Asia). Three principles, all added after watching v1 fail to progress:

  1. Dense win signal first. Stage 0 is 1v1 against a single nation on Onion; stage 1 is 1v3. Only then do tribe bots and full nation rosters appear. Before this, "win the game" was so rare the win bonus never fired.
  2. Map pools, not single maps. Each stage samples its map per episode from a pool, so the agent can't overfit one layout.
  3. Rehearsal against forgetting. 25% of episodes replay a map from an earlier (cleared) stage at the current stage's bot count and difficulty — old maps come back harder. Rehearsal episodes train the policy but don't count toward advancement.

Advancement is win-gated: rolling win rate over the last 40 on-stage episodes must exceed 0.5 — the agent must be winning more often than not, not merely surviving.

Performance engineering

GPU util was poor and env stepping was the bottleneck. Five fixes, one afternoon:

FixWhy it mattered
Per-batch dynamic grid paddingEverything was padded to the max map (75×125 regions); Onion is 32×32 — roughly 9× wasted conv compute on small maps, plus VRAM. Now obs stay native-size and collate pads to the batch max.
Double-buffered rolloutEnv stepping (CPU, Node) and policy inference (GPU) were serial. Group A steps while group B is on the GPU — the pipeline hides whichever side is cheaper.
bf16 autocast + TF32 + fused AdamWFree speed on the 5090; --compile flag for torch.compile when warm-up cost is acceptable.
Binary tile IPCTile grids crossed the bridge as gzip+base64 JSON every step. Now a JSON header + raw bytes on stdout; Python reads exact lengths. Cut serialization CPU on both sides.
Per-stage decision_ticksEarly stages decide every 15 engine ticks instead of 10 — fewer policy round-trips where fine control isn't needed yet.

Visualization & tooling

Determinism is the superpower here: the bridge mirrors the client's createGameRunner() init exactly (same PseudoRandom ID stream, no spawn timer), so an agent game saved as a GameRecord replays bit-identically in the real client, and verify_record.ts proves it by re-simulating from intents alone.

ToolWhat it does
rl/watch.pyOne greedy episode → flat-palette WebM with a debug side panel, plus the GameRecord and a .debug.json sidecar (every decision: action, probs, value).
scripts/render_client_replay.pyHeadless Chromium replays the record in the actual game client → WebM with real graphics. Agent renders as AGENT with the gold spawn ring, the crown when first, and the "You Won!" modal; camera starts fit to the whole map.
scripts/serve_replay.pyArchive-API shim so a normal browser can replay agent records interactively (serves /game/<id> and /debug/<id>).
MODEL overlay (RlDebugOverlay.ts)In-client panel synced to the sim tick: chosen action, value estimate, probability bars, recent-action log. Works in rendered videos, browser replays, and live matches — it auto-probes the agent's debug feed on localhost:8988.
rl/play.py + bridge/play.tsThe agent joins a real lobby over the live websocket protocol; you fight it from the normal client while watching it think.
patches/client-replay-tooling.patchAll client hooks (replayViewAs, replayFitMap, tick export, overlay) as an uncommitted-submodule patch, auto-reapplied by the render script if the submodule is reset.

Bug war stories

Wins were never counted. vec.py checked winner[1] == "Agent" (the username); the engine emits the clientID ("AGENTRL1"). Every "the agent never wins" observation before Jul 6 12:06 was partly measurement error. Found only by watching a replay where the agent visibly won while the stats said otherwise. Moral: validate the reward path end-to-end with a game you can see.
Case-insensitive macOS hid a Linux crash. Map directory lookup lowercased only the first character (BlackSea → blackSea); the dirs are fully lowercase. Worked on every Mac test, exploded on the pods with ENOENT.
Resume logic fought the supervisor. The pod supervisor passed a stale --stage 4; resume took max(cli, checkpoint), so a manually reset checkpoint kept snapping back — worse, a still-running trainer kept re-saving its old stage over the reset. Fixes: checkpoint stage is authoritative, and kill the whole process group (kill -9 $(pgrep -f "[r]l\.ppo")) before editing checkpoints.
Cropped-map index crash. Transport/nuke target coordinates weren't bounds-checked against the 16px-cropped grid; a boat targeting the trimmed edge strip of BlackSea produced an IndexError deep in the obs builder.
Headless-browser environment quirks. Vite binds IPv6 ::1 by default while headless Chromium resolves localhost to IPv4 — force --host 127.0.0.1. And the client hard-rejects software WebGL; Chromium needs --use-angle=metal --enable-gpu --ignore-gpu-blocklist on macOS.

Lessons distilled

Only compress what's big. The AE is for the map. Exact small state rides the bypass. This single decision dissolved a week's worth of planned loss-weight tuning.
Detection beats regression on sparse targets. Structure counts on 99.9%-empty grids collapse to zero under MSE; rarity-weighted BCE occupancy hits P/R 1.0.
Make wins reachable before making them valuable. A win bonus is useless if the setting never produces wins. 1v1 → 1v3 staging turned the win signal from theoretical to dense, and win-gating advancement keeps the bar honest.
Rehearse or forget. Revisit cleared maps at current difficulty for a fixed fraction of episodes; don't let stage N erase stages 0..N−1.
Exploit determinism everywhere. Bit-identical replay gives verification (verify_record), visualization (real-client videos), and live mirroring (play.ts) from one mechanism.
Pad to the batch, not the maximum. Dynamic padding + global↔local index conversion for pointer heads: most of a "GPU too slow" problem was actually wasted convolution.
Watch the agent play. The replay/debug tooling isn't garnish — it caught the win-detection bug, the camera problems, and makes reward-hacking visible in a way curves never will.
Treat cloud pods as disposable. Checkpoint everything (optimizer, stage, rolling stats), sync to HF periodically, restore on boot, supervise with auto-restart. Every ops incident so far was some violation of this.

Infrastructure

Five RTX 5090 RunPod pods at time of writing: rl2 (ppo_v2c), rl3 (ppo_v3), and three AE/BC experiment pods. scripts/pod_train.sh is the supervisor (idempotent deploy, auto-restart, keeps sshd alive as a start command); scripts/runpod_bootstrap.sh bootstraps a fresh pod end-to-end (repo, deps, both datasets prefeaturized). Checkpoints and datasets live on Hugging Face: openfront-snapshots, openfront-tile-autoencoder, plus the RL checkpoint repo. Local git history was scrubbed once with git filter-repo after large blobs snuck in — data dirs are gitignored now.
